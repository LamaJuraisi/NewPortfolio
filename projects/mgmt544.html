MGMT 544 - Fresh Produce Management Insights

Your guide to data-driven freshness, inventory efficiency, and smart restocking.

Objective

Partnered with Fresh Harvest Grocery to design a database that tracks produce freshness and minimizes waste. With this system, they can say goodbye to expired stock and hello to better sales!

Business Scenario & Problem

Fresh Harvest Grocery, a local store, struggled with inventory loss due to expired produce. They lacked an effective alert system to notify employees when products were nearing expiration, leading to restocking issues, lost sales, and additional costs. Our database solution aimed to address these challenges by providing real-time data for better decision-making.

Key Insights from Our Analysis

  • Proactive Inventory Management: Using our average shelf life analysis, Fresh Harvest can identify product categories with shorter shelf lives, allowing for quicker turnover and reducing waste.
  • Employee Accountability: By identifying which employees handle restocking, Fresh Harvest can ensure accountability, improving efficiency in stock management.
  • Supplier Selection for Freshness: With insights into which suppliers deliver the longest-lasting produce, Fresh Harvest can select partners that align with their freshness standards.

Key Code Snippets

Average Shelf Life by Product Category


SELECT P.Category, AVG(DATE_DIFF(PI.PurchaseDate, PI.ExpirationDate, DAY)) AS AvgShelfLife
FROM FreshHarvestDB.ProductInventory AS PI
JOIN FreshHarvestDB.Product AS P ON P.ProductID = PI.ProductID
GROUP BY P.Category;

Recommendation: Categories with the shortest shelf life should be monitored closely, restocked frequently, and promoted to reduce potential waste.

Employee Responsible for Restocking a Specific Product


SELECT E.EmployeeID, E.EmployeeFirstName_, E.EmployeeLastName, 
       P.ProductID, P.Category_
FROM FreshHarvestDB.Product AS P 
JOIN FreshHarvestDB.Employee AS E ON P.Employee_ID = E.EmployeeID
WHERE P.ProductID = 1027;

Recommendation: Fresh Harvest can use this data to track and assign accountability, ensuring stock is handled efficiently.

Supplier with Longest Shelf Life for Produce


SELECT s.Supplier_Name, 
       AVG(DATE_DIFF(PI.PurchaseDate, PI.ExpirationDate, DAY)) AS Avg_Freshness_At_Arrival
FROM FreshHarvestDB.Supplier s
JOIN FreshHarvestDB.Purchase pu ON s.SupplierID = pu.SupplierID
JOIN FreshHarvestDB.Product p ON pu.ProductID = p.ProductID
JOIN FreshHarvestDB.ProductInventory PI ON pu.ProductID = PI.ProductID
WHERE p.Category_ IN ('fruits', 'vegetables', 'dairy')
GROUP BY s.Supplier_Name
ORDER BY Avg_Freshness_At_Arrival;

Recommendation: Fresh Harvest should prioritize suppliers with the longest shelf life to maintain freshness and reduce spoilage.
