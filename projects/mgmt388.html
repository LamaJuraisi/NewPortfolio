MGMT 388 - Coffee Trade Analysis

A technical exploration of coffee trade data, predictive modeling, and insights

Objective

The goal of this project was to explore the fascinating world of coffee trade, diving into data from 2002 to 2023 to uncover hidden patterns, trends, and insights. By combining data analytics and machine learning, we aimed to answer questions like: How do coffee imports change over time? What drives these changes? and What can we predict about the future of coffee trade? Ultimately, this project celebrates the intersection of numbers and storytelling—one cup of data at a time.

Process Summary

  • Analyzed global coffee trade data.
  • Explored seasonal trends and country-specific contributions.
  • Applied regression models to predict coffee imports.
  • Clustered data for advanced insights.

Project Insights

Significant market patterns revealed the interconnectedness of production and consumption trends globally.

Did you know? Coffee imports in the US increased by 45% from 2002 to 2023, reflecting evolving consumer preferences.

Tools & Techniques

  • Python: Libraries like pandas, seaborn, and statsmodels for analysis and modeling.
  • Visualizations: Used matplotlib and plotly for dynamic and interactive visualizations.

Key Code Snippets

Regression Analysis

# Linear Regression for Predicting Imports
import statsmodels.api as sm

X = us_data[['Arabica_Production', 'Year', 'Domestic_Consumption']]
y = us_data['Bean_Imports']
X = sm.add_constant(X)
model = sm.OLS(y, X).fit()
print(model.summary())

Interpretation: The regression model showed an R-squared of 0.86, indicating strong predictive power. Key predictors were statistically significant at the 5% level.

After analyzing production trends, we turned our focus to domestic consumption in the US. Understanding these patterns is key to aligning import strategies with demand.

Results

  • Regression Analysis: The model explained 86% of the variance, highlighting the importance of Arabica production, year, and domestic consumption in predicting imports.
  • Time-Series Analysis: Seasonal trends revealed peaks in coffee imports during winter months, likely influenced by increased holiday demand.
  • Cluster Analysis: Segmentation grouped countries by import volume, offering actionable insights for supply chain optimization.
