MGMT 388 - Coffee Bean Distribution

Analyzing coffee trade across 94 countries from 1960 to 2023

Objective

To analyze coffee distribution trends across 94 countries, focusing on production, imports, and exports. The project aimed to uncover insights using regression analysis and visualization techniques to highlight key patterns.

Process Summary

  • Explored a USDA dataset detailing coffee supply and distribution from 1960 to 2023.
  • Cleaned the dataset by removing inconsistent records and irrelevant columns.
  • Performed regression analysis to study domestic consumption trends in the United States.
  • Visualized top exporters and importers globally, focusing on Arabica and Robusta beans.

Key Insights

  • Arabica dominates global production, with the United States as the largest importer.
  • Robusta has shown steady growth, with Vietnam as the leading producer.
  • Analyzing trends post-2002 provided more consistent insights due to data anomalies in earlier years.

Results

  • Regression analysis revealed that increased domestic consumption correlates strongly with imports in the US.
  • Data cleaning and visualization highlighted the importance of consistent timeframes for accurate analysis.
  • Arabica and Robusta remain the most significant contributors to global coffee trade.

Next Steps

  • Incorporate additional economic indicators like GDP and trade tariffs for deeper insights.
  • Develop machine learning models to forecast coffee trade trends.
