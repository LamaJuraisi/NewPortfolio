MGMT 388 - Coffee Trade Analysis

A technical exploration of coffee trade data, predictive modeling, and insights

Objective

The goal of this project was to explore the fascinating world of coffee trade, diving into data from 2002 to 2023 to uncover hidden patterns, trends, and insights. By combining data analytics and machine learning, we aimed to answer questions like: How do coffee imports change over time? What drives these changes? and What can we predict about the future of coffee trade? Ultimately, this project celebrates the intersection of numbers and storytellingâ€”one cup of data at a time.

Process Summary

  • Analyzed global coffee trade data.
  • Explored seasonal trends and country-specific contributions.
  • Applied regression models to predict coffee imports.
  • Clustered data for advanced insights.

Project Insights

Significant market patterns revealed the interconnectedness of production and consumption trends globally.

Did you know? Coffee imports in the US increased by 45% from 2002 to 2023, reflecting evolving consumer preferences.

Tools & Techniques

Regression Analysis


# Linear Regression for Predicting Imports
import statsmodels.api as sm

X = us_data[['Arabica_Production', 'Year', 'Domestic_Consumption']]
y = us_data['Bean_Imports']
X = sm.add_constant(X)
model = sm.OLS(y, X).fit()
print(model.summary())

Interpretation: The regression model showed an R-squared of 0.86, indicating strong predictive power. Key predictors were statistically significant at the 5% level.

Regression Analysis: Predicting US Coffee Imports

As the largest single-country importer of coffee beans, the US provides a unique dataset for predictive analysis. By examining data from 2002 to 2023, we identified key predictors such as production, domestic consumption, and time.
