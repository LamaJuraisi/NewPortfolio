MGMT 474 - Stock Market Analysis

Exploring stock performance for Nike, Adidas, and Puma

Objective

This project aimed to dive into the dynamics of the stock market, focusing on three iconic brands: Nike, Adidas, and Puma. Through machine learning and data visualization, we explored questions like: How do these brands' stock prices move together? and What trends can we predict for future investments? This journey was about more than numbersâ€”it was about uncovering the stories behind the stocks.

Process Summary

  • Collected stock market data for Nike, Adidas, and Puma from reputable financial sources.
  • Cleaned and preprocessed data to ensure accuracy and consistency.
  • Applied various machine learning models to identify trends and predictive insights.
  • Visualized findings through R-generated plots to enhance understanding.

Project Insights

Our data-driven approach revealed significant market trends. By leveraging machine learning models, we optimized decision-making processes for investors and portfolio managers.

Did you know? Stocks like Nike, Adidas, and Puma often exhibit synchronized movements, making them ideal candidates for portfolio diversification.

Tools & Techniques

  • R Programming: Extensively used for analysis and visualization.
  • Regression Analysis: Identified relationships between stock prices.
  • KNN and Random Forest: Used for predictive modeling with high accuracy.

Random Forest Regression


# Random Forest Regression
library(randomForest)
rf_model <- randomForest(StockPrice ~ ., data = train_data, ntree = 500)
rf_predictions <- predict(rf_model, test_data)
rmse <- sqrt(mean((rf_predictions - test_data$StockPrice)^2))

Random Forest Regression Results

Interpretation: The Random Forest model achieved a very low RMSE, indicating high predictive accuracy. This method proved effective for modeling stock price trends.

Results

  • Random Forest: Achieved the highest accuracy for predictive modeling, outperforming other methods.
  • Regression Analysis: Highlighted strong correlations between Nike and Adidas stock prices.
  • Naive Bayes: Showed moderate performance, indicating room for improvement in modeling stock movements.
